# MONTHS 1–2: SWITCH-READY

## Week 1–2 — Foundation + assessment (don't apply yet)
- **DSA (daily):** Arrays, strings, hashing, two pointers, sliding window. ~3–4 problems/day. Time yourself.
- **System design:** Watch/read to get the *vocabulary* you already practice (Hello Interview + ByteByteGo). Write **one deep-dive of your own DR system** as a portfolio narrative — multi-tenancy, partition strategy, Redis caching, RabbitMQ pipeline. This doubles as interview gold.
- **Resume:** Rewrite to impact bullets. Format: *"Cut report latency 45s → <1s by fixing partition pruning across 120+ tenant schemas."* Numbers + verb + impact. 1–2 pages.
- **Behavioral:** Brain-dump 8–10 stories: Redis 24MB key crisis, stampede protection, partition pruning win, DB purging task, mentoring 2–3 juniors, RabbitMQ KT. Raw notes only for now.

## Week 3–4 — Momentum + START APPLYING
- **DSA (daily):** Trees, linked lists, stacks/queues, recursion, binary search. Hold the daily streak.
- **System design:** 2–3 classics (URL shortener, rate limiter, news feed). Map your real experience onto each — you've *built* rate limiting and caching, say so.
- **LLD:** Refresh SOLID + 5–6 patterns with code (Strategy, Factory, Observer, Decorator, Repository).
- **APPLY — this is the week.** Don't wait to be "ready":
  - Referrals first (ex-colleagues, LinkedIn network at JPMC/Walmart/Microsoft + product cos)
  - Then portals
  - Aim 5–8 quality applications/week, referral-led

## Week 5–8 — Interview cycle for GCCs / product cos
- **DSA (daily):** Graphs, intervals, heaps, intro DP. The harder patterns.
- **Mocks:** Start now. Pramp / interviewing.io / a peer. 1–2 mocks/week (alternate DSA + system design). This is where most of the real improvement happens.
- **Interview live.** Treat early interviews as paid mocks. After each rejection, write down the exact gap and patch it that week.
- **Goal by end of Month 2:** a live pipeline + ideally an offer at GCC/product level. That alone hits ₹28–35 LPA.

# MONTHS 3–6: FAANG-READY

You can keep interviewing/switch in parallel — a GCC offer in hand makes you negotiate FAANG from strength.

- **DSA:** Advanced DP, graph algorithms (Dijkstra/union-find/topo), tries, occasionally segment trees. Goal: **mediums fluently, hards approachable.** This is the FAANG bar and it's mostly volume + spaced repetition.
- **System design (staff-level):** Harder, scale-first — design Twitter, Uber, a distributed cache, a notification system. Lean hard on your Redis/K8s/RabbitMQ depth; push into *tradeoffs* (CAP, consistency models, hot-key handling — you literally fixed a hot-key crisis).
- **Behavioral at FAANG bar:** Tighten STAR stories to the specific company (Amazon LPs if Amazon, scope/impact framing for staff). Practice narrating ownership and ambiguity.
- **Apply:** Targeted FAANG + referrals. Use the GCC/product interview reps as your warmup.
